Initiative

Iniative was a concept visual design for a tabletop RPG companion app. The app would include features like automated dice rolling, dynamically-populated character sheets for any gaming system (Dungeons & Dragons v. Pathfinder v. Deadlands), and a graphical inventory with item tracking for things like currency, ammunition, etc.

Tooling

I used Sketch for most of the mocking up and prototyping, Adobe Illustrator for creating iconography and other vector assets, and Photoshop for all photo mocks, etc.

Typography

San Francisco UI is the system font for iOS devices. Originally I had been working on an Android version of the mockup, and was using Roboto as the main font, but when I switched to the iOS version, SF was the way to go.

Color & Typography

I wanted to keep the color use simple and minimal throughout the app, adhering almost exclusively to use of a single super gradient or primary color.

In Re:

This was a labor of love, mostly. I’m a huge fan of tabletop RPG gaming, and saw a place in the community for a really smooth, comprehensive, easy-to-use companion app to ease with menial mathematical/bookkeeping tasks that can sometimes detract from the roleplay experience.

Ultimately, I’d love to build out Initiative. I think there’s still a place for it in the market, and I think it’d be a welcome entry in the tabletop community. Until then, it was a really fun foray into app design, and a great introduction to design tools like Sketch that I wasn’t very familiar with.
